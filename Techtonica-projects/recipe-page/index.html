Colombian Empanadas 🇨🇴

My recipe project for Techtonica is inspired by a trip I took to Colombia last month. While there, I ate some of the best food of my entire life! I visited my friend's family ranch in a small town called Acacias, where I had TRUE farm to table meals. I tried my first Colombian empanadas in Acacias, made with rice and meat. When I traveled to the capitol, Bogota, I tried empanadas made with potatoes and beef. Empanadas in Colombia are typically served with ají sauce. 10/10 would recommend! 😍

Ingredients for the Filling

  • Vegetables: Potatoes, white onions, scallions, red bell pepper, tomato and garlic.
  • Spices: Sazón with azafran, chicken vegetable bouillon, salt and black pepper.
  • Oil: Olive oil
  • Herbs: Fresh cilantro or parsley.
  • Meat: Ground beef and pork.

Ingredients for the Dough

  • Masarepa: Yellow precooked cornmeal.
  • Water: Lukewarm
  • Oil: I recommend using vegetable or canola oil.
  • Spices: Sazón with azafran and salt.

Making the dough

  1. To make the dough, place the masarepa in a large bowl.
  2. Add the sazón and salt, and stir to mix well.
  3. Add the water and oil and mix to form dough.
  4. Pat the dough into a ball and knead for 2 minutes or until smooth.
  5. Cover with plastic and set aside for 20 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile make the filling.

Making the filling

  1. To make the filling, place the potatoes in a pot with water and the bouillon tablet for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Drain and gently mash the potatoes. Set aside.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a skillet. Add the onion and cook over medium-low heat stirring frequently, for 5 minutes. Add the tomatoes, green onions, garlic, bell pepper, cilantro, salt and black pepper. Cook for about 15 minutes.
  3. Add the ground pork and beef. Cook, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon, for 10 to 15 minutes or until the mixture is fairly dry.
  4. Transfer the meat mixture to the mashed potatoes bowl and mix well to combine.

Assembling and cooking the empanadas

  1. Break small portions of the dough, about 1 ½ tablespoons each one, and form each portion into a ball by rolling between the palms of your hands.
  2. Place the balls of dough between two pieces of plastic and roll each out very thinly to form a circle. Remove the top plastic and place 1 tablespoon of the filling in the center of each.
  3. Then using the plastic underneath, fold the dough over to enclose the filling, forming a half circle. Tightly seal the edges by crimping with the tines of a fork.
  4. Fill a large pot with vegetable oil and heat over medium heat to 360° F.
  5. Carefully place 3 or 4 empanadas at the time in the heated oil and fry for about 2 minutes until golden on all sides. Using a slotted spoon transfer the empanadas to a plate lined with paper towels.
